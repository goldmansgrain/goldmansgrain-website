The Story

Real Wood. Real Reasons.

I started woodworking because I was tired of "handmade" products that fell apart in two years. You'd buy something marketed as artisan, put it on a shelf, and six months later the edges were peeling and the finish was chipping off the MDF underneath.

Real wood doesn't do that. Real wood gets better. It picks up character. It survives kids, spills, and life — and it still looks good on the shelf when everything else has been thrown away.

Every single piece I make — signs, name boards, toys, decor — is cut, sanded, and finished from real hardwood. Maple, walnut, cherry, and oak, sourced for their grain, their weight, and the way they'll look 20 years from now.

The Materials

Four Hardwoods. Zero Compromises.

Each wood is chosen for its character, its grain, and the way it ages. Here's what you'll find in every Goldman's Grain piece.

Hard Maple

Light, fine-grained, and incredibly durable. Hard maple is the go-to for toys, signs, and kitchen pieces — it takes detail work beautifully and ages to a warm honey tone.

Black Walnut

Deep, rich, and unmistakable. Walnut is the luxury hardwood — heavy, strong, and stunning. Used for kitchen decor, serving pieces, and heirloom toys that look like art.

Black Cherry

Warm reddish-brown that deepens with age and sunlight. Cherry is the sleeper hardwood — it starts beautiful and gets better every year, developing a rich patina over time.

White Oak

Pronounced grain, golden tone, and serious strength. White oak is the classic American hardwood — bold, characterful, and built for pieces that need to stand up to real life.
