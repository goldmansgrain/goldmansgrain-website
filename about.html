The Story

Real Wood. Built to Last Generations.

I started building kids' toys because I was fed up. Walk into any store and you'll find "wooden" toys made from MDF, particle board, or cheap pine held together with glue and hope. They look fine on a shelf. Give them to a two-year-old and see how long they last.

Real hardwood is different. Maple, walnut, cherry, oak — these are materials that get better over time. They pick up character. They survive kids, siblings, spills, and the general chaos of childhood. And when it's all over, they still look good enough to pass down.

That's what I'm building at Goldman's Grain. A rocking horse your kid will hand to their kid. A tool set carved from solid walnut that a toddler today might pull out of a box for their own children someday. Building blocks so solid and beautiful that parents keep them out as decor when the kids are done playing.

Everything is made to order in Raleigh, NC — cut, sanded, and finished by hand. No warehouse inventory. No shortcuts. Just real wood built for real life, and built to outlast it.

The Materials

Four Hardwoods. Zero Compromises.

Each wood is chosen for its character, grain, and durability — the way it ages, the way it feels in a child's hand, and the way it looks 40 years from now.

Hard Maple

Light, fine-grained, and incredibly durable. The go-to for toys, signs, and kitchen pieces — takes detail work beautifully and ages to a warm honey tone. Safe, hard, and smooth.

Black Walnut

Deep, rich, and unmistakable. Walnut is the luxury hardwood — heavy, strong, and stunning in toys like tool sets and building blocks that need to feel serious in little hands.

Black Cherry

Warm reddish-brown that deepens with age and sunlight. Cherry gets better every year, developing a rich patina over time — a wood that visibly earns its heirloom status.

White Oak

Pronounced grain, golden tone, and serious strength. White oak is the classic American hardwood — bold, characterful, built to stand up to kids, kitchens, and real life.
