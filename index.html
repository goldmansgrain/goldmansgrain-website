Why Goldman's Grain

Toys That Outlast the Trend. And the Tantrum.

Most kids' toys are made from MDF, particle board, and plastic — they look fine for a year and fall apart by two. Goldman's Grain was built on a single rule: if it isn't solid hardwood, it doesn't leave the shop.

A rocking horse made from solid maple. A tool set carved from solid walnut. Building blocks in contrasting walnut and maple. These aren't toys that go in a landfill — they're heirlooms that get handed down. Your kids play with them. Your grandkids play with them. That's the whole point.
