Every Grain Tells a Different Story

Four premium hardwoods, each with its own character, tone, and feel. Drag the slider to compare them side by side.

Maple White Oak Walnut Cherry

Hard Maple

Light, fine-grained, and incredibly durable. The go-to for toys, signs, and nursery pieces — takes detail work beautifully and ages to a warm honey tone over decades. Safe, hard, and smooth to the touch.

Tone Light Cream → Warm Honey
Character Fine grain, tight texture
Best for Toys, name signs, trivets

White Oak

The most versatile hardwood in the shop. White oak sits right between maple and walnut — warm tan with a golden undertone, open grain, and the iconic ray fleck figure that sets it apart. Strong, stable, and beautiful in any finish.

Tone Warm Tan → Golden Honey
Character Open grain, ray fleck figure
Best for Trays, risers, home accents

Black Walnut

Deep, rich, and unmistakable. The luxury hardwood — heavy, strong, and stunning. The dark chocolatey grain pops under a hand-rubbed finish and only gets richer with time. We never use stains to fake this look.

Tone Dark Chocolate
Character Bold grain, rich figure
Best for Tool sets, trays, risers

Black Cherry

Warm reddish-brown that deepens with age and sunlight. Cherry gets more beautiful every year, developing a rich amber patina — a wood that visibly earns its heirloom status the longer it sits in your home.

Tone Reddish-Brown → Deep Amber
Character Smooth, even grain
Best for Book rests, home decor

Why Goldman's Grain
